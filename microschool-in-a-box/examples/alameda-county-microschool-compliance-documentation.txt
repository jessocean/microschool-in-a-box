SECTION A: STATE-LEVEL REQUIREMENTS
==================================

A.1 SCHOOL LICENSING REQUIREMENTS
---------------------------------

PRIVATE SCHOOL AFFIDAVIT FILING
Legal Requirement: Private schools must file affidavit annually between October 1-15 [https://www.cde.ca.gov/sp/ps/rq/psprivateschools.asp; "Filing Requirements" section]

Compliance Criteria:
- Annual filing between October 1-15 (mandatory window) [https://www.cde.ca.gov/sp/ps/rq/psprivateschools.asp; "When to File" section]
- Must cover all students ages 6-18 in the facility [Cal. Ed. Code § 33190; https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=33190&lawCode=EDC; subsection (a)]
- Online submission through CDE website required [https://www3.cde.ca.gov/psa; login portal instructions]
- Schools established after October 15 must file immediately upon establishment [https://www.cde.ca.gov/sp/ps/rq/psprivateschools.asp; "Late Filing" section]
- No filing fee [https://www.cde.ca.gov/sp/ps/rq/psprivateschools.asp; "Fees" section]

CURRICULUM REQUIREMENTS
Legal Requirement: Must offer "instruction in the several branches of study required to be taught in public schools" [Cal. Ed. Code § 51210; https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=51210&lawCode=EDC; subsection (a)]

Compliance Criteria:
- Full-time educational program required for students ages 6-18 [Cal. Ed. Code § 48222; https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=48222&lawCode=EDC; subsection (a)]
- No specific state curriculum mandated for private schools [https://www.cde.ca.gov/sp/ps/rq/psprivateschools.asp; "Curriculum Requirements" section]
- No state approval of curriculum required [https://www.cde.ca.gov/sp/ps/rq/psprivateschools.asp; "State Oversight" section]

A.2 EMPLOYMENT LAW REQUIREMENTS
-------------------------------

BACKGROUND CHECK AND FINGERPRINTING
Legal Requirement: All employees with student contact must submit fingerprints [Cal. Ed. Code § 44237; https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=44237&lawCode=EDC; subsection (a)]

Compliance Criteria:
- Two sets of fingerprints required for DOJ and FBI criminal record checks [Cal. Ed. Code § 44237; https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=44237&lawCode=EDC; subsection (b)]
- DOJ provides summary within 15 working days (3 days with electronic system) [Cal. Ed. Code § 44237; https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=44237&lawCode=EDC; subsection (c)]
- Information stored in locked file separate from other files [Cal. Ed. Code § 44237; https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=44237&lawCode=EDC; subsection (d)]
- Information destroyed upon hiring determination [Cal. Ed. Code § 44237; https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=44237&lawCode=EDC; subsection (d)]
- Must check for: sex offenses, controlled substance offenses, crimes of violence, serious/violent felonies [Cal. Ed. Code § 44237; https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=44237&lawCode=EDC; subsection (a)]

WORKERS COMPENSATION INSURANCE
Legal Requirement: Mandatory for all employers with one or more employees [Cal. Labor Code § 3700; https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=3700&lawCode=LAB; subsection (a)]

Compliance Criteria:
- Coverage required for all employees including part-time [Cal. Labor Code § 3351; https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=3351&lawCode=LAB; definition section]
- Must obtain certificate of insurance [Cal. Labor Code § 3700; https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=3700&lawCode=LAB; subsection (b)]
- Coverage maintained continuously during employment periods [Cal. Labor Code § 3700; https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=3700&lawCode=LAB; subsection (a)]

A.3 BUSINESS REGISTRATION REQUIREMENTS
-------------------------------------

LLC FORMATION
Legal Requirement: Must register with California Secretary of State [Cal. Corp. Code § 17701.04; https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=17701.04&lawCode=CORP; subsection (a)]

Compliance Criteria:
- File Articles of Organization online at bizfileonline.sos.ca.gov [https://bizfileonline.sos.ca.gov/; main filing portal]
- Registration fee: $70 [https://www.sos.ca.gov/business-programs/business-entities/fees; LLC formation fee schedule]
- Additional taxes and fees: $820 due within first 4 months [Cal. Rev. & Tax. Code § 17941; https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=17941&lawCode=RTC; subsection (b)]

FEDERAL AND STATE TAX REGISTRATION
Legal Requirement: Federal EIN and California tax registration required [26 U.S.C. § 6109; https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/6109; subsection (a)]

Compliance Criteria:
- Obtain EIN from IRS (no fee) [https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online; application portal]
- Register with California Franchise Tax Board [https://www.ftb.ca.gov/file/business/types/llc-partnership/index.html; registration requirements]
- Register with CDTFA if applicable for sales tax [https://www.cdtfa.ca.gov/registration/; registration portal]

A.4 TAX OBLIGATIONS
------------------

FRANCHISE TAX
Legal Requirement: Annual minimum franchise tax $800 for LLCs [Cal. Rev. & Tax. Code § 23153; https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=23153&lawCode=RTC; subsection (a)]

Compliance Criteria:
- Annual payment required regardless of income [Cal. Rev. & Tax. Code § 23153; https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=23153&lawCode=RTC; subsection (a)]
- Due date: 15th day of 4th month after beginning of tax year [Cal. Rev. & Tax. Code § 23153; https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=23153&lawCode=RTC; subsection (c)]
- Mandatory fee: $800 annually [https://www.ftb.ca.gov/file/business/types/llc-partnership/index.html; fee schedule section]

PROPERTY TAX EXEMPTIONS (OPTIONAL)
Legal Requirement: Private schools may qualify for welfare exemption [Cal. Rev. & Tax. Code § 214; https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=214&lawCode=RTC; subsection (a)]

Compliance Criteria for Exemption:
- Property used exclusively for educational purposes [Cal. Rev. & Tax. Code § 214; https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=214&lawCode=RTC; subsection (a)(1)]
- Must qualify as 501(c)(3) or 501(c)(4) [Cal. Rev. & Tax. Code § 214; https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=214&lawCode=RTC; subsection (d)]
- No part of net earnings may benefit private individuals [Cal. Rev. & Tax. Code § 214; https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=214&lawCode=RTC; subsection (g)]

A.5 BUILDING AND FIRE SAFETY REQUIREMENTS
-----------------------------------------

BUILDING CODE OCCUPANCY CLASSIFICATION
Legal Requirement: Educational use classifies as Group E occupancy [Cal. Building Code Ch. 3, Section 303; https://up.codes/viewer/california/ca-building-code-2019/chapter/3/occupancy-classification-and-use#303; Group E definition]

Compliance Criteria:
- Group E classification for educational component [Cal. Building Code § 303.1; https://up.codes/viewer/california/ca-building-code-2019/chapter/3/occupancy-classification-and-use#303.1; educational occupancy definition]
- Group R classification for residential components [Cal. Building Code § 310; https://up.codes/viewer/california/ca-building-code-2019/chapter/3/occupancy-classification-and-use#310; residential occupancy definition]
- Mixed occupancy requirements apply [Cal. Building Code § 508; https://up.codes/viewer/california/ca-building-code-2019/chapter/5/general-building-heights-and-areas#508; mixed use provisions]
- Maximum occupancy load calculations required [Cal. Building Code § 1004.5; https://up.codes/viewer/california/ca-building-code-2019/chapter/10/means-of-egress#1004.5; Table 1004.5]

FIRE SAFETY SYSTEMS
Legal Requirement: Mixed occupancy buildings require automatic sprinkler systems [Cal. Fire Code § 903.2; https://up.codes/viewer/california/ca-fire-code-2019/chapter/9/fire-protection-and-life-safety-systems#903.2; sprinkler requirements]

Compliance Criteria:
- Automatic sprinkler system required for mixed Group E/Group R occupancy [Cal. Fire Code § 903.2.3; https://up.codes/viewer/california/ca-fire-code-2019/chapter/9/fire-protection-and-life-safety-systems#903.2.3; Group E requirements]
- Sprinkler system must comply with NFPA 13 standards [Cal. Fire Code § 903.3.1.1; https://up.codes/viewer/california/ca-fire-code-2019/chapter/9/fire-protection-and-life-safety-systems#903.3.1.1; NFPA 13 reference]
- Fire safety and evacuation plans required [Cal. Fire Code § 404.2; https://up.codes/viewer/california/ca-fire-code-2019/chapter/4/emergency-planning-and-preparedness#404.2; emergency evacuation plan requirements]

B.1 ZONING REQUIREMENTS
----------------------

CONDITIONAL USE PERMIT REQUIREMENT
Legal Requirement: Educational facilities in residential zones require Conditional Use Permit [Alameda County Code Title 17, Chapter 17.54; https://library.municode.com/ca/alameda_county/codes/code_of_ordinances?nodeId=TIT17ZO_CH17.54ZOPR_17.54.070COUSPE; conditional use permit requirements]

Compliance Criteria:
- Submit CUP application to Alameda County Planning Department [Alameda County Code § 17.54.070; https://library.municode.com/ca/alameda_county/codes/code_of_ordinances?nodeId=TIT17ZO_CH17.54ZOPR_17.54.070COUSPE; application procedures]
- Public hearing required before Board of Zoning Adjustments [Alameda County Code § 17.54.080; https://library.municode.com/ca/alameda_county/codes/code_of_ordinances?nodeId=TIT17ZO_CH17.54ZOPR_17.54.080PUHECO; hearing requirements]
- Demonstrate use will not be detrimental to neighborhood [Alameda County Code § 17.54.070(D); https://library.municode.com/ca/alameda_county/codes/code_of_ordinances?nodeId=TIT17ZO_CH17.54ZOPR_17.54.070COUSPE; approval criteria]
- Processing time: typically 2-3 months [Contact Planning Department for current processing times: (510) 670-5400]

R-1 DISTRICT RESTRICTIONS
Legal Requirement: Single-family residential districts have specific use restrictions [Alameda County Code § 17.08.030; https://library.municode.com/ca/alameda_county/codes/code_of_ordinances?nodeId=TIT17ZO_CH17.08R-SI_17.08.030USPE; permitted uses]

Compliance Criteria:
- Front yard setback: 20 feet minimum [Alameda County Code § 17.08.050; https://library.municode.com/ca/alameda_county/codes/code_of_ordinances?nodeId=TIT17ZO_CH17.08R-SI_17.08.050YARE; front yard requirements]
- Side yard requirements: 5 feet minimum [Alameda County Code § 17.08.050; https://library.municode.com/ca/alameda_county/codes/code_of_ordinances?nodeId=TIT17ZO_CH17.08R-SI_17.08.050YARE; side yard requirements]
- Must meet parking requirements for educational facilities [Alameda County Code § 17.56.040; https://library.municode.com/ca/alameda_county/codes/code_of_ordinances?nodeId=TIT17ZO_CH17.56PARE_17.56.040NUPARE; parking ratios]
- Hours of operation may be restricted [Contact Planning Department for specific restrictions: (510) 670-5400]

Compliance Criteria:

- Fire evacuation plan required
- Fire safety inspection required
- Emergency egress requirements must be met

LOCAL BUSINESS PERMIT

B.4 FOOD SERVICE PERMITS
----------------------------------------

FOOD FACILITY PERMIT
Legal Requirement: All facilities serving food require environmental health permits [California Health & Safety Code § 114381; reference provided in state requirements above]

Local Implementation by Alameda County Environmental Health Department:
- Annual permit and inspection required [Contact (510) 567-6858 for current requirements]
- Plan review process for new facilities [Contact for plan review procedures]
- Commercial kitchen requirements must be met [Contact for specific kitchen code requirements]

Penalty Structure:
- Payments must be received within 30 days to avoid penalties [Contact Environmental Health for current penalty structure]
- 25% penalty assessed after 30 days
- Additional 25% penalty assessed after 60 days
- Annual fees are non-refundable and non-transferable

B.5 OTHER LOCAL REQUIREMENTS
----------------------------

FOOD SAFETY CERTIFICATION
Local Requirement: Food handlers must complete certification through Alameda County [Contact Environmental Health Department: (510) 567-6858]

Compliance Criteria:
- Complete food safety certification class (offered in English and Spanish)
- Certification required for all food handling staff
- Classes available through Alameda County Environmental Health

SECTION C: COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST
==============================

TIMELINE: 4-6 months from start to opening
TOTAL ESTIMATED FEES: Contact agencies for current amounts (fees change regularly)

PHASE 1: FOUNDATIONAL REQUIREMENTS (Months 1-2)

□ Step 1: Establish Business Entity (Processing time: immediate online)
  Dependencies: None
  Fee: $70 registration + $820 additional taxes/fees within 4 months
  Agency: California Secretary of State - (916) 653-6814
  Source: [https://bizfileonline.sos.ca.gov/; main filing portal]

□ Step 2: Obtain Federal Employer Identification Number (Processing time: immediate online)
  Dependencies: Complete Step 1
  Fee: No fee
  Agency: Internal Revenue Service
  Source: [https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online; application portal]

□ Step 3: Register for State Taxes (Processing time: 1-2 weeks)
  Dependencies: Complete Step 2
  Fee: No registration fee
  Agency: California Franchise Tax Board - (800) 852-5711
  Source: [https://www.ftb.ca.gov/file/business/types/llc-partnership/index.html; registration requirements]

□ Step 4: Contact Alameda County Planning Department (Processing time: immediate)
  Dependencies: None
  Fee: No fee for consultation
  Agency: Alameda County Planning Department - (510) 670-5400, Planninginfo@acgov.org
  Source: [https://www.acgov.org/cda/planning/; contact information]

PHASE 2: PERMITS AND LICENSES (Months 2-4)

□ Step 5: Apply for Conditional Use Permit (Processing time: 2-3 months)
  Dependencies: Complete Step 4
  Fee: Contact Planning Department for current CUP fees
  Agency: Alameda County Planning Department - (510) 670-5400
  Source: [Alameda County Code § 17.54.070; application procedures]

□ Step 6: Apply for Building Permit (Processing time: 1-2 months)
  Dependencies: Should coordinate with Step 5, requires CUP approval
  Fee: Contact Building Department for current fees
  Agency: Alameda County Building Department (contact through Planning Department)
  Source: [Alameda County Building Code; change of use requirements]

□ Step 7: Install Fire Safety Systems (Processing time: 2-4 weeks)
  Dependencies: Required before Certificate of Occupancy
  Fee: Contact contractors for installation costs
  Agency: Fire Marshal approval required
  Source: [Cal. Fire Code § 903.2; sprinkler requirements]

□ Step 8: Obtain Business License (Processing time: 1-2 weeks)
  Dependencies: Complete Step 1
  Fee: Contact county or city for current annual fees
  Agency: Alameda County (unincorporated areas) or appropriate city
  Source: [Alameda County Business License Ordinance]

□ Step 9: Contact Environmental Health Department (Processing time: immediate)
  Dependencies: None
  Fee: No fee for consultation
  Agency: Alameda County Environmental Health - (510) 567-6858, dehwebbilling@acgov.org
  Source: [https://www.acgov.org/aceh/index.htm; contact information]

□ Step 10: Apply for Food Facility Permit (Processing time: 2-4 weeks)
  Dependencies: Complete Step 9
  Fee: Contact Environmental Health for current permit fees
  Agency: Alameda County Environmental Health - (510) 567-6858
  Source: [Cal. Health & Safety Code § 114381; permit requirements]

□ Step 11: Obtain Food Safety Certification (Processing time: 1 day class)
  Dependencies: Required before food facility permit approval
  Fee: Contact Environmental Health for class fees
  Agency: Alameda County Environmental Health - (510) 567-6858
  Source: [Contact Environmental Health for certification requirements]

PHASE 3: FINAL APPROVALS (Months 4-6)

□ Step 12: Conduct Background Checks for Staff (Processing time: 15 working days)
  Dependencies: Required before hiring staff with student contact
  Fee: Varies by fingerprinting provider
  Agency: California Department of Justice - (916) 227-3849
  Source: [Cal. Ed. Code § 44237; background check requirements]

□ Step 13: Obtain Workers Compensation Insurance (Processing time: immediate)
  Dependencies: Required before hiring any employees
  Fee: Varies by insurance provider
  Agency: Licensed insurance carrier
  Source: [Cal. Labor Code § 3700; workers comp requirements]

□ Step 14: Develop Curriculum (Processing time: ongoing)
  Dependencies: Must be completed before school operation
  Fee: No mandatory fees
  Agency: No state approval required
  Source: [Cal. Ed. Code § 51210; curriculum requirements]

□ Step 15: Schedule Final Inspections (Processing time: 1-2 weeks coordination)
  Dependencies: Complete construction, fire safety, and food service preparations
  Fee: May include inspection fees
  Agency: Building Department, Fire Marshal, Environmental Health
  Source: [Various code requirements for final approvals]

□ Step 16: File Private School Affidavit (Processing time: immediate online)
  Dependencies: School must be established and operational
  Fee: No filing fee
  Agency: California Department of Education - (916) 319-0800
  Source: [https://www3.cde.ca.gov/psa; filing portal]

ONGOING REQUIREMENTS:

□ Step 17: Pay Ongoing Annual Fees and Maintain Compliance
  Dependencies: Ongoing requirement once operational
