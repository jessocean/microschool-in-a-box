SACRAMENTO COUNTY ONE-ROOM SCHOOLHOUSE MICROSCHOOL COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST (HIGH CAPACITY)
===================================================================

Parameters: 1 teacher, 20 students ages 5-14, no parents present, food served, residential dwelling
Date Created: August 30, 2025

COMPLETE THESE STEPS IN ORDER TO OPEN A MICROSCHOOL IN SACRAMENTO COUNTY, CALIFORNIA:

1. ESTABLISH BUSINESS ENTITY
   - File Articles of Organization for LLC with California Secretary of State online at bizfileOnline.sos.ca.gov
   - Pay registration fee: $70
   - Pay additional taxes and fees: $820 (due within first 4 months of formation)
   - Dependency: Must be completed before obtaining EIN and other business registrations

2. OBTAIN FEDERAL EMPLOYER IDENTIFICATION NUMBER (EIN)
   - Apply for EIN from IRS (no fee, can be done online)
   - Dependency: Requires completed LLC formation from Step 1

3. REGISTER FOR STATE TAXES
   - Register with California Franchise Tax Board
   - Register with California Department of Tax and Fee Administration if applicable
   - Dependency: Requires EIN from Step 2

4. VERIFY JURISDICTIONAL BOUNDARIES AND ZONING
   - Determine if property is in unincorporated Sacramento County or within city limits
   - If in city limits, contact appropriate city for requirements instead of county
   - Contact Sacramento County Planning Department at (916) 874-6141 or planning@saccounty.gov
   - Verify specific zoning district (AR Agricultural Residential, R-1 Single Family, etc.)
   - Office: 827 7th Street, Room 225, Sacramento, CA 95814
   - Hours: Monday-Friday 8:00am-5:00pm
   - Dependency: Must be completed before applying for conditional use permit

5. CONTACT SACRAMENTO COUNTY PLANNING DEPARTMENT FOR DETAILED REQUIREMENTS
   - Call (916) 874-6141 or email planning@saccounty.gov
   - Confirm conditional use permit requirements for educational facility in your specific zoning district
   - Obtain current CUP application fees and processing timeline (typically 3-4 months)
   - Review parking requirements (minimum 1 space per teacher/staff, additional 2-3 spaces for drop-off/pick-up for 20-student capacity)
   - Review signage restrictions (typically 4-6 square feet maximum in residential zones)
   - Confirm requirement for traffic impact study (REQUIRED for 20-student facility)
   - Dependency: Requires completion of Step 4

6. APPLY FOR CONDITIONAL USE PERMIT (CUP)
   - Submit conditional use permit application to Sacramento County Planning Department
   - Include site plan and operational details for 20-student capacity
   - Demonstrate compatibility with residential neighborhood character
   - Address traffic, noise, and parking impacts for 20-student operation
   - Include required traffic impact study
   - Processing time: typically 3-4 months
   - Public hearing required before Planning Commission
   - Notice requirements to surrounding property owners
   - Pay mandatory CUP application fees (contact Planning Department for current amount)
   - Dependency: Requires completion of Step 5

7. CONTACT SACRAMENTO COUNTY BUILDING DEPARTMENT FOR BUILDING PERMIT REQUIREMENTS
   - Call (916) 874-6222
   - Address: 827 7th Street, Room 101, Sacramento, CA 95814
   - Review building permit requirements for change of use (residential to mixed educational/residential)
   - Determine fire safety system requirements for 21-occupant facility
   - Review ADA accessibility compliance requirements
   - Obtain current building permit fees
   - Determine fire department jurisdiction for your property location
   - Online: www.saccounty.gov/buildingpermits
   - Dependency: Should be done concurrently with Step 5, requires CUP progress

8. APPLY FOR BUILDING PERMIT (CHANGE OF USE)
   - Submit building permit application to Sacramento County Building Department
   - Include architectural plans showing educational use areas for 20 students
   - Show compliance with Group E/Group R mixed occupancy requirements
   - Include occupancy load calculations (400 sq ft minimum classroom space for 20 students at 20 sq ft per student)
   - Show ADA accessibility compliance (including 1 accessible parking space)
   - Include fire safety systems installation plans (sprinkler and alarm systems REQUIRED for 21 occupants)
   - Include structural analysis if required for occupancy load changes
   - Pay building permit fees (contact Building Department for current amount)
   - Dependency: Requires CUP approval from Step 6

9. INSTALL REQUIRED FIRE SAFETY SYSTEMS (MANDATORY FOR 20-STUDENT CAPACITY)
   - Install automatic sprinkler system (REQUIRED for mixed Group E/Group R occupancy with 20+ students)
   - Ensure sprinkler system complies with NFPA 13 standards
   - Install fire alarm system (REQUIRED based on 21-occupant load)
   - Install emergency egress improvements (proper door hardware, width, swing direction)
   - Install exit signs and emergency lighting (REQUIRED for 20+ occupant facilities)
   - Develop fire safety and evacuation plans for 21 occupants
   - Contact appropriate fire department for jurisdiction:
     * Sacramento Metropolitan Fire District: (916) 859-4300 (for some areas)
     * Contact Building Department for fire department referral
   - Dependency: Required before Certificate of Occupancy, must coordinate with building permit process

10. OBTAIN SACRAMENTO COUNTY BUSINESS LICENSE
    - Determine if property is in unincorporated Sacramento County or city limits
    - For unincorporated areas: Contact Sacramento County Business License at (916) 874-7000
    - For city areas, contact appropriate city:
      * City of Sacramento: (916) 808-7100, www.cityofsacramento.org
      * City of Elk Grove: (916) 478-3635
      * City of Folsom: (916) 355-7203
      * City of Rancho Cordova: (916) 851-8700
    - Apply for business license with required documentation
    - Pay annual business license fee (contact appropriate jurisdiction for current amount)
    - Maintain license annually and display at business location
    - Online Sacramento County: www.saccounty.gov/business
    - Dependency: Requires business entity formation from Step 1

11. CONTACT SACRAMENTO COUNTY ENVIRONMENTAL MANAGEMENT FOR FOOD SERVICE REQUIREMENTS
    - Call (916) 875-8484 or email EMDInfo@saccounty.gov
    - Address: 10590 Armstrong Avenue, Suite B, Mather, CA 95655
    - Obtain current food facility permit fees and requirements for 20-student capacity
    - Review commercial kitchen requirements for schools serving 20 students
    - Schedule plan review process for food service operation
    - Determine food safety manager certification requirements
    - Review three-compartment sink and handwashing facility requirements for 20-student capacity
    - Online: www.emd.saccounty.gov
    - Hours: Monday-Friday 8:00am-5:00pm
    - Dependency: Must be completed before applying for food facility permit

12. APPLY FOR FOOD FACILITY PERMIT
    - Submit food facility permit application to Sacramento County Environmental Management Department
    - Complete plan review process for new food facilities serving 20 students
    - Ensure commercial kitchen requirements are met (commercial-grade three-compartment sink, handwashing facilities, proper food storage for 20 students)
    - Pay application fees, plan review fees, and annual permit fees (contact Environmental Management for current amounts)
    - Schedule initial food facility inspection
    - Note: Re-inspection fees may apply for failed inspections
    - Dependency: Requires completion of Step 11

13. OBTAIN FOOD SAFETY MANAGER CERTIFICATION
    - At least one supervisory person must obtain food safety manager certification
    - Certification must be from ANSI-accredited program (ServSafe, National Registry of Food Safety Professionals)
    - Maintain certification and renew as required
    - Certificate must be available for inspection during operations
    - Dependency: Required before food facility permit approval and ongoing operation

14. ADDRESS SPECIAL RURAL CONSIDERATIONS (IF APPLICABLE)
    - For properties with private well water: Contact Environmental Management for well water testing requirements
    - For properties with septic systems: Evaluate septic system capacity for increased usage (20 students + 1 teacher - septic upgrade likely required)
    - For agricultural preserve areas: Contact Planning Department to review Williamson Act contract restrictions
    - For rural road access: Contact Sacramento County Department of Transportation at (916) 874-6292 for access requirements and encroachment permits (road improvements likely required for 20-student capacity)
    - Dependency: Varies by property location and characteristics

15. CONDUCT BACKGROUND CHECKS FOR ALL STAFF
    - Submit two sets of fingerprints for each employee with student contact to California Department of Justice
    - Include DOJ and FBI criminal record checks
    - Processing time: DOJ provides summary within 15 working days (3 days with electronic system)
    - Background check must cover: sex offenses, controlled substance offenses, crimes of violence, serious/violent felonies
    - Store information in locked file separate from other files
    - Destroy information upon hiring determination
    - Pay fingerprinting and background check processing fees (varies by provider)
    - Contact: California Department of Justice at (916) 227-3849
    - Dependency: Must be completed before hiring any staff with student contact

16. OBTAIN WORKERS COMPENSATION INSURANCE
    - Purchase workers compensation insurance coverage from licensed carrier
    - Obtain certificate of insurance
    - Maintain coverage continuously during all employment periods
    - Coverage required for all employees including part-time
    - Dependency: Required before hiring any employees

17. DEVELOP CURRICULUM AND EDUCATIONAL PROGRAM
    - Create instruction program covering "several branches of study required to be taught in public schools"
    - Ensure full-time educational program for students ages 6-18
    - Provide instruction comparable to public school requirements
    - Note: No specific state curriculum mandated for private schools
    - Note: No state approval of curriculum required
    - Dependency: Must be completed before school operation begins

18. SCHEDULE FINAL INSPECTIONS AND OBTAIN OCCUPANCY APPROVAL
    - Schedule final building inspection with Sacramento County Building Department
    - Schedule final fire safety inspection with appropriate fire department (enhanced inspection required for sprinkler and alarm systems)
    - Schedule final food facility inspection with Environmental Management Department for 20-student capacity kitchen
    - Obtain Certificate of Occupancy from Building Department for 21-occupant mixed use facility
    - Dependency: Requires completion of all construction, fire safety, and food service preparations

19. FILE PRIVATE SCHOOL AFFIDAVIT WITH CALIFORNIA DEPARTMENT OF EDUCATION
    - File annually between October 1-15 at www3.cde.ca.gov/psa
    - Note: Schools established after October 15 must file immediately upon establishment
    - Note: Schools established in July must wait until August to file
    - Cover all 20 students ages 6-18 in the facility
    - Online submission required through CDE website
    - No filing fee
    - General inquiries: (916) 319-0800
    - Dependency: Can only be filed after school is established and operational

20. PAY ONGOING ANNUAL FEES AND TAXES
    - Pay California LLC annual franchise tax: $800 minimum (due 15th day of 4th month after beginning of tax year)
    - Renew Sacramento County or city business license annually
    - Renew food facility permit annually with Environmental Management Department
    - File Private School Affidavit annually (October 1-15) for 20 students
    - Maintain workers compensation insurance continuously
    - Maintain food safety manager certification
    - Dependency: Ongoing requirement once school is operational

OPTIONAL ACCREDITATION (NOT LEGALLY REQUIRED):
21. PURSUE WASC ACCREDITATION (OPTIONAL)
    - Apply to Western Association of Schools and Colleges (WASC)
    - Meet WASC eligibility requirements
    - Employ qualified teaching personnel
    - Implement systematic school improvement process
    - Focus on student achievement aligned with academic standards
    - Pay application fees (contact WASC at www.acswasc.org for current amounts)
    - Note: UC course approval requires WASC accreditation or candidate status
    - Note: Improves student transfer opportunities and higher education acceptance
    - Dependency: Can be pursued after school is operational and stable

ESTIMATED TOTAL TIMELINE: 5-8 months from start to opening (extended due to traffic study and enhanced fire safety requirements)
CRITICAL PATH: Conditional Use Permit (3-4 months), traffic impact study (additional 1-2 months), and building modifications/permits with mandatory fire safety systems

KEY DIFFERENCES FROM LOW CAPACITY (12-STUDENT) VERSION:
- Traffic impact study is MANDATORY for 20-student facilities
- Fire safety systems (sprinklers and alarms) are REQUIRED, not optional
- Commercial-grade kitchen equipment required for 20-student food service
- Enhanced parking requirements (2-3 additional spaces vs 1-2 for 12-student)
- Septic system upgrade likely required for rural properties
- Road improvements likely required for rural access
- Extended timeline due to additional requirements
- Enhanced review process for traffic, fire safety, and food service capacity

- Building Permit: Contact Building Department for current fees (higher due to fire safety system requirements)
- Food Facility Permit: Contact Environmental Management for current fees
